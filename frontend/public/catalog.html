DIVG is a decentralised platform that verifies impact-investing claims to fight impact washing. It runs verification once through a peer-prediction validator network (Compact SPP; Witkowski & Parkes, 2012), then issues a reusable Verified Impact Claim — minted on SUI, with evidence and the full audit trail anchored on Walrus, and an optional Hedera Consensus Service log.

Why decentralisation

Impact verification today is gatekept by centralised intermediaries — auditors and rating agencies — who can be captured, can extract rent, and form single points of failure and trust. DIVG treats verification as a public good: it redistributes the authority to verify away from any single gatekeeper toward a transparent network of stakeholders, where honest reporting is the rational strategy (Compact SPP; Witkowski & Parkes, 2012) and the evidence, the votes, and the result are independently auditable on-chain. The contribution is structural — decentralisation as a redistribution of trust-making power — not a claim that any one actor is more honest, but that no one actor has to be trusted.
